Who I am...

As a communicator of science and adventure, I find myself as a student to my surroundings. Currently, as a student at the University of Wisconsin Madison, studying GIS Development I am learning and developing new ways to communicate both adventure and science. As an avid back country skier, climber, & cyclist, using maps is something that I do everyday. Using geospatial technology fuels my everyday adventure and curiosity. I aspire to be a communicator of complex science using geospatial technology. In years past, I have been heavily involved in water resources, specifically studying the hydrological impact of beavers on the Milwaukee River Watershed. This work has been successfully propelling me into various postions, including Team Lead at the National Centers of Environmental Information (NCEI) with NASA DEVELOP. As a second term DEVELOPer I have aquired the skills to use NASA Earth Observations in order to guide in agricultural decision making in the Midwest and soon in the Mato Grosso state of Brazil.
